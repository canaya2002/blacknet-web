Most "X vs Y" posts in social management read like marketing material. This one tries to be honest. We have operated Hootsuite, Sprout Social, and Blacknel side by side across 40+ accounts for the last six months. Here is what we learned.

## Pricing reality check

| | Hootsuite | Sprout Social | Blacknel |
|---|---|---|---|
| Entry plan | $99/mo (Professional) | $249/mo (Standard) | $69/mo (Standard) |
| Mid-tier | $249/mo (Team) | $399/mo (Advanced) | $299/mo (Growth) |
| Enterprise | Quote | Quote | $1,099/mo (or quote) |
| Per-user adders | $30-50/seat | Yes | $19/seat or included |
| White label | Enterprise only | Enterprise add-on | Included on Enterprise |

The headline: Hootsuite is cheapest to enter, most expensive once you add seats. Sprout Social is most consistent at mid-market but never cheap. Blacknel is positioned to undercut both with a transparent enterprise ceiling.

## Where each one wins

### Hootsuite wins at: legacy enterprise inertia

If your company already has 50 seats on Hootsuite and a multi-year contract, switching is hard. Hootsuite knows this and has stopped innovating accordingly. It is fine, not great, and predictable.

### Sprout Social wins at: enterprise listening and CRM-style workflows

Sprout has the most mature listening product among the three. If your team has a dedicated PR / comms function that needs deep keyword monitoring with custom dashboards, Sprout is hard to beat — at a price.

### Blacknel wins at: agencies and multi-location brands needing AI + reviews

Blacknel built around the inverse premise of the incumbents: that AI should be native, that reviews and reputation belong in the same plane as social inbox, and that agencies running 10+ brands need real multi-tenant RBAC from day one. If that describes you, the migration math is brutal.

## Migration costs nobody talks about

Switching tools is not just a feature comparison. It is:

- **Retraining your team.** Plan 2-3 weeks of degraded productivity per replatform.
- **Re-onboarding clients.** White-label dashboards mean updating client logins and saved bookmarks.
- **Re-establishing approval workflows.** Each tool encodes these slightly differently.
- **Re-importing brand voice memory.** Most tools do not export AI training data, so you start from zero.

Plan for one full quarter of "we are switching, please be patient" before the new tool out-performs the old one. Anyone who promises faster is selling.

## Our honest take

If you are an agency under 25 clients, a multi-location chain, or a DTC brand that values transparent pricing and AI that respects your tone: try Blacknel.

If you are an enterprise PR team needing deep listening with custom dashboards and no price sensitivity: Sprout is still the safe pick.

If you are stuck on Hootsuite with a multi-year contract: budget for migration in the year your contract ends. The longer you wait, the harder the migration becomes.

Either way, demand a real demo with your actual brands loaded. The "polished demo on stock content" tells you nothing.
